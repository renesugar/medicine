This site publishes clinical decision-support frameworks and narrative articles on translational medicine.

## Purpose

Clinicians frequently encounter patients with microvascular symptoms — head pressure, sensory distortion, cognitive slowing, post-exertional malaise — that are invisible to standard investigations. This site provides:

- **Diagnostic frameworks** grounded in the latest mechanistic research
- **Treatment algorithms** that are sequenced by pathophysiology, not symptom
- **Lab panels** with specific codes and action thresholds for real-world ordering
- **Interactive tools** for bedside clinical decision-making

## Scope

Current content covers:

- The **platelet-serotonin axis** in SSRI discontinuation syndrome
- The **endothelial-fibrin axis** in Long COVID / PASC
- The **four-step pathophysiological model**: endothelium → platelets → fibrin → fibrinolysis
- **Vitamin K2**, the vWF–ADAMTS13 axis, platelet microparticles, and fibrinolytic failure

## Disclaimer

> All content on this site is intended for educational and clinical-discussion purposes only. It does not constitute medical advice or a prescription. All treatment decisions must be made by a licensed clinician with full knowledge of the individual patient's history, contraindications, and risk factors. No clinical trials have validated these protocols as complete systems.

## Adding Content

This site uses Jekyll. New articles can be added in:

- **Markdown format** — place a `.md` file in `articles/` with YAML front matter
- **HTML format** — place a standalone HTML file in `articles/`

See the [sample markdown article](articles/sample-markdown-article.html) for a full template.
